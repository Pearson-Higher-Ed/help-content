How Do I Upload Content?

When you have personal content that you have created and want to include in a Collection, you can upload it. Your content must be a PDF file that is no greater than 30MB. To preview a resource after you upload it, click its title.

When you upload content, Pearson Collections makes inquiries about the copyright of the material. If you are the holder of the copyright, you can upload and use the content immediately once you supply the necessary information.

If you don't hold the copyright, Pearson can help you acquire it for a Collection. When this is done, Pearson tries to acquire rights for the submitted content.

Important: Pearson Collections requires a class of 100 students in order to upload content.

  1. Click Browse to locate the PDF file you want to upload.
  2. Once the file uploads, enter the information about the file. This text will appear as the title of the content.
  3. (Optional) Add the name of the person who created the content.
  4. Do one of the following:
    1. If you own the necessary rights to the content, select Yes, I have the necessary right to use and publish this content. Proceed to step 6.
    2. If you don't own the necessary rights to the content, select No, I need assistance obtaining rights for this content . Proceed to step 5.
  5. Enter the requested information. When you finish click Done.
  6. The uploaded content is added to your Collection. You can move it up or down in the Collection just like any other resource. You can also submit the Collection for printed or publication, but it won't be printed or approved for student digital viewing until Pearson has acquired rights for you.
